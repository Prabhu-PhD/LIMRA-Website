FMGE 2026: How to Prepare and What to Expect

L LIMRA Coaching Team

The Foreign Medical Graduate Examination (FMGE) is the gateway every Indian student who studies medicine abroad must pass to practise back home. It has a reputation for being tough — the national pass rate often sits below 20% — but with a clear plan and disciplined revision, it is very much beatable. Here's how to approach FMGE 2026.

What the exam actually looks like

The FMGE is conducted by the National Board of Examinations (NBE), typically twice a year. The essentials to plan around:

  • 300 multiple-choice questions across all 19 medical subjects
  • 150 marks (50%) needed to pass — there is no negative marking
  • Two papers covering pre-clinical, para-clinical and clinical subjects
  • Held twice a year, so you can plan your attempt around your internship

Because every subject can appear, breadth matters as much as depth. The goal is reliable coverage of all 19 subjects, not mastery of a few.

Build a subject-wise plan

Group the syllabus and give each block a realistic window. High-yield subjects — Pathology, Pharmacology, Medicine, Surgery, Obstetrics & Gynaecology and Community Medicine (PSM) — deserve the most time, while smaller subjects can be revised in focused bursts. Write the plan down with dates so it becomes a schedule, not a wish.

Practise with mock tests — relentlessly

FMGE rewards exam temperament. Regular full-length mocks train you to manage 300 questions in the available time, flag your weak areas, and get comfortable with the NBE question style. Just as important as taking the mock is reviewing it: every wrong answer is a gap you can close before the real exam. LIMRA students sit 50+ mocks with detailed answer discussions for exactly this reason.

Time your preparation

Start early and revise often. A workable rhythm is a first pass through all subjects, a second tighter pass on high-yield topics, and a final phase that is almost entirely mock tests plus targeted revision of your weakest areas. Short, daily study beats occasional marathons.

A quick checklist

  • Cover all 19 subjects — don't gamble on selective study
  • Prioritise high-yield subjects for the bulk of your time
  • Take full-length mocks regularly and review every one
  • Revise actively — recall, don't just re-read
  • Use a structured coaching program to stay accountable
Exam dates, eligibility and the screening framework (including the planned transition to NExT) can change. Always confirm the current FMGE schedule and rules on the official NBE notice and with your LIMRA coaching coordinator.
