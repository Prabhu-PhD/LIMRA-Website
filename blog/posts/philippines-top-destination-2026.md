For more than two decades, the Philippines has been the first choice for Indian students who want to become doctors but find domestic seats scarce and private fees out of reach. In 2026, that momentum is stronger than ever. Here's an honest look at why — and what to weigh before you apply.

## 1. English is the medium from day one

Unlike many overseas destinations, the Philippines teaches medicine entirely in English. There's no foreign language to learn before you can follow lectures, read textbooks or talk to patients. For Indian students, that removes one of the biggest hidden hurdles of studying abroad and lets you focus on medicine itself.

## 2. An NMC-aligned, US-style curriculum

Philippine medical programs follow a familiar structure: a pre-medical phase (BS course) followed by the Doctor of Medicine (MD). The leading universities design their curricula to align with India's National Medical Commission (NMC) norms and are recognised by bodies such as the WHO directory — important if you intend to return and practise in India.

## 3. Real clinical exposure, early

Strong Philippine schools attach students to teaching hospitals for hands-on training rather than keeping them in lecture halls. Early, supervised patient contact builds the clinical confidence that matters both for your degree and for the screening exam back home.

## 4. Affordable — without donations

Cost is often the deciding factor. Tuition and living expenses in the Philippines are a fraction of private medical college fees in India, and reputable universities do not charge the large capitation "donations" common elsewhere. That makes a recognised medical degree genuinely accessible for middle-class families.

## 5. A clear pathway to FMGE success

A foreign degree only counts in India once you clear the Foreign Medical Graduate Examination (FMGE). The right university — paired with structured coaching — makes that far more achievable. LIMRA's students clear the FMGE at a 92% rate, well above the national average, supported by free in-house coaching worth ₹1,00,000.

### In short, students choose the Philippines for:

- English-medium teaching with no language barrier
- NMC-aligned curricula at recognised universities
- Early, real clinical training in teaching hospitals
- Transparent, affordable fees with no donations
- A proven route to clearing the FMGE and practising in India
