Eligibility & NEET

Do I need to qualify NEET to study MBBS abroad?

Yes. As per NMC Regulations (amended 2024), all Indian nationals must qualify NEET before enrolling in any MBBS programme abroad. You must meet the qualifying percentile — 50th for General category, 40th for SC/ST/OBC.

What is the minimum NEET score required?

The NMC prescribes a qualifying percentile, not a fixed score. For 2024–25, the General category cutoff was approximately 137+ marks. The actual mark corresponding to this percentile varies year to year based on exam difficulty and the total candidate pool.

What is the age limit for MBBS abroad?

You must be at least 17 years old at the time of admission. There is no NMC-prescribed upper age limit for studying MBBS abroad, though individual universities may have their own guidelines — our counsellors can confirm this for each institution.

Can NRI or OCI students apply?

Yes. NRI and OCI students are eligible. They must still clear NEET under General category norms and meet all standard NMC eligibility criteria for overseas MBBS.

The MBBS Programme

How many years does the MBBS programme take?

At LIMRA’s Philippine partner universities (DMSF, Brokenshire, GCM, Lyceum NW), the programme is 5.5 years — 4.5 years of academics followed by a 1-year compulsory internship. At the Timor-Leste universities (UCTS, UNPAZ), the programme is 6 years including internship.

What is the medium of instruction?

English is the sole medium of instruction at all six LIMRA partner universities. Lectures, textbooks, exams, clinical teaching, and patient interaction are all in English — so there is no language barrier for Indian students.

Are the universities recognised by NMC and WHO?

Yes — all six LIMRA partner universities are listed in the World Directory of Medical Schools (WDOMS) and are recognised by India’s National Medical Commission. NMC recognition is a mandatory requirement for Indian graduates to appear for FMGE/NExT, and all our partners meet this criterion.

What expenses should I budget for beyond tuition?

In addition to tuition, plan for hostel / accommodation (approximately ₹1–2 lakh per year), food (roughly ₹5,000–8,000 per month), return flights twice a year, and incidentals. LIMRA provides a detailed itemised cost sheet for each university at no charge during your free counselling session.

FMGE, NExT & Returning to India

Can I practise medicine in India after completing MBBS abroad?

Yes. Once you clear FMGE/NExT and complete a 12-month compulsory rotating internship at an NMC-approved Indian hospital, you receive full NMC registration and can practise anywhere in India — including sitting for NEET-PG to pursue a postgraduate specialisation.

What is the FMGE / NExT exam?

FMGE (Foreign Medical Graduate Examination) is a licensing test conducted by the National Board of Examinations for Indian citizens who completed MBBS abroad. It covers 19 medical subjects. From 2025, it is being phased into NExT (National Exit Test) — a single common gateway for all medical graduates regardless of where they studied in India or abroad.

What is LIMRA’s FMGE coaching track record?

LIMRA has been conducting FMGE coaching since 2001 and operates South India’s largest coaching programme. Over 2,000 students have cleared FMGE/NExT through LIMRA coaching. In the January 2026 examination alone, 200+ LIMRA-coached students were successful.

When should I start FMGE preparation?

Ideally from the 4th year of your MBBS abroad. Starting early allows integrated learning alongside your clinical years. LIMRA offers structured online modules and intensive holiday batches designed for students in the Philippines and Timor-Leste, so you arrive back in India already well prepared.

Practical Matters

What is the approximate total cost for MBBS in the Philippines?

The total all-inclusive cost (tuition + hostel + living expenses) for 5.5 years at LIMRA’s Philippine partner universities typically ranges between ₹25–35 lakh, depending on the university and lifestyle. This is substantially less than private medical colleges in India, which often exceed ₹80 lakh. LIMRA provides a precise, itemised breakdown for each university at no charge.

How is the food and climate for Indian students?

Indian mess facilities and grocery stores are available near all LIMRA partner campuses. The Philippines has a tropical climate very similar to South India — warm and humid with a dry season from November to May. Timor-Leste has a warm, dry tropical climate. Most Indian students adapt comfortably within the first few weeks.
