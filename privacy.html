Privacy Policy

Last updated: 17 June 2026

LIMRA Overseas Education ("LIMRA", "we", "our", or "us") is committed to protecting the privacy of every student, parent, and visitor who interacts with us. This Privacy Policy explains what personal data we collect, why we collect it, how we use and protect it, and the rights you have over it. We process personal data in accordance with India's Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (DPDP Act) and other applicable laws.

This policy applies to www.limraedu.com and to enquiries you make with us by web form, phone, WhatsApp, email, or in person. By sharing your information with us, you consent to the practices described here.

1. Information We Collect

We only collect the data we need to respond to you and provide our services:

  • Information you give us: your name, phone number, email address, area of interest (e.g. MBBS Philippines, FMGE coaching), and anything else you choose to include in a message or during counselling.
  • Information collected automatically: when you browse our website we may collect limited technical data — IP address, browser and device type, pages visited, and referring page — through cookies and analytics tools. This is used in aggregate and is not used to personally identify you.
  • Records of our communication: notes from counselling calls, WhatsApp or email correspondence, and the documents you voluntarily share for admission guidance.

We do not ask you to submit sensitive financial information (such as card or bank details) through this website.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use your personal data only for clear, legitimate purposes:

  • To contact you about your enquiry by phone, WhatsApp, or email, and to provide counselling and guidance on overseas medical education and FMGE coaching.
  • To assist with university applications, admissions, and related processes you ask us to help with.
  • To send you relevant updates about admissions, deadlines, coaching schedules, and services you have shown interest in. You can opt out of these at any time.
  • To improve our website and understand, in aggregate, how visitors use it.
  • To meet legal, accounting, or regulatory obligations.

3. Legal Basis & Consent

Under the DPDP Act, we process your personal data on the basis of the consent you give when you submit an enquiry or otherwise contact us, and for the legitimate purposes connected to the services you request. You may withdraw your consent at any time (see Your Rights below); withdrawing consent will not affect processing already carried out lawfully.

4. Cookies & Analytics

Our website uses cookies and similar technologies to function correctly and to measure traffic. We may use analytics services (such as Google Analytics) that set cookies to report on how visitors use the site. These reports are aggregated and do not identify you personally. You can block or delete cookies in your browser settings; some parts of the site may work less smoothly if you do.

5. Data Sharing & Third-Party Processors

We do not sell or rent your personal data. We share it only where necessary to operate our services, and only with providers bound to protect it:

  • Web3Forms — processes submissions from our website forms and delivers them to our inbox.
  • Netlify — hosts this website and may process basic server and request data.
  • Google Analytics — provides aggregated website-usage statistics.
  • WhatsApp / telecom providers — used when you choose to contact us, or we contact you, through those channels.
  • Partner universities and institutions — where you ask us to assist with an application, we share the details necessary to process it with the relevant university (which may be located outside India — see below).

We may also disclose data where required by law, court order, or a lawful request from a public authority.

6. International Data Transfers

Because we help students secure admission to universities abroad (including in the Philippines and Timor-Leste), the data you ask us to submit for an application may be transferred to and processed by institutions outside India. We share only what is necessary for the purpose you have requested, and we take reasonable steps to ensure your data is handled responsibly.

7. Data Security

We apply reasonable technical and organisational safeguards to protect your personal data against loss, misuse, and unauthorised access. However, no method of transmission over the internet is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

8. Data Retention

We keep your personal data only for as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes set out in this policy — typically for the duration of our engagement with you and a reasonable period afterwards — unless a longer period is required by law. When data is no longer needed, we securely delete or anonymise it.

9. Children's Data

Some of our prospective students may be below 18 years of age. In line with the DPDP Act, we process a child's personal data only with the consent of a parent or lawful guardian, and we do not knowingly use children's data in any way likely to cause them harm. If you are under 18, please involve your parent or guardian when sharing information with us.

10. Your Rights

Subject to the DPDP Act, you have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you and request a summary of how it is processed.
  • Request correction or updating of inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Request erasure of your personal data where it is no longer required.
  • Withdraw your consent to processing at any time.
  • Nominate another individual to exercise your rights in the event of death or incapacity.
  • Raise a grievance with us, and escalate to the Data Protection Board of India if unresolved.

To exercise any of these rights, contact our Grievance Officer using the details below. We will respond within a reasonable time.

11. Third-Party Links

Our website may link to external sites (for example, partner universities or social media). We are not responsible for the privacy practices or content of those sites; we encourage you to read their privacy policies.

12. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or the law. The "Last updated" date at the top shows when it was last revised. Significant changes will be highlighted on this page.

13. Grievance Officer & Contact
